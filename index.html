2ndStage Partners helps early stage and second stage companies efficiently increase the velocity of revenue growth in order to achieve business maturity.

We know we work well together, and we know we work well separately, having individually spent decades on the frontlines of dozens of successful growth companies, startups, mergers, acquisitions and shareholder liquidity events. We’re on a mission--we want to help companies grow (and grow quickly!) by offering our unique combination of individual experience and collective success.

We see a huge gap in entrepreneurial support in America today. There is lots of help for startups, but the skills and expertise that make a successful startup don’t necessarily translate to rapid, efficient growth to and through the second stage. We know how to do it. Let us help you.

Larry Freed, Managing Partner

Prior to founding 2nd Stage Partners, Larry Freed was the founder and CEO of the B2B technology firm ForeSee, the world leader in customer experience analytics. Larry led the company from inception in 2001 to a successful exit in 2013 that returned over 22 times the capital originally invested in the business while also growing revenue every quarter since the company was founded. During his tenure at ForeSee, Larry assembled a world-class executive leadership team, many of whom he has brought on to join him at 2nd Stage Partners.

Prior to founding ForeSee, Larry held several senior executive positions at Compuware Corporation, leading a variety of e-commerce and professional services programs. He served as Vice President of e-Business, where he was responsible for directing the national e-Business practice as well as the design, development, and implementation of e-Business strategies for numerous companies. Before joining Compuware, Larry was CTO at Bank One.

Larry is the author of two books, Managing Forward (2011) and the national best-seller Innovating Analytics (2013). Larry is a top rated speaker and innovator on the way we look at the customer experience across all touch points and at the brand level. Dedicating an enormous body of research to improving customer satisfaction analytics across all disciplines for over a decade, Larry and ForeSee developed the Word of Mouth Index (WoMI) and the ForeSee Experience Index. He has been quoted in numerous publications and media including CNN, the Wall Street Journal, the New York Times, Fortune, Forbes, the Washington Post, Tech Crunch, Mashable, and Investor’s Business Daily, as well as hundreds of other trade and local media.

Larry is currently on the University of Michigan Technology Transfer National Advisory Board and previously held board and leadership positions with various other industry groups. Larry was an Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year Finalist and a Digital Analytics Association Most Influential Industry Contributor Finalist. In addition, under Larry’s leadership, ForeSee won various awards and recognitions such as Ann Arbor Business Review Company of the Year, seven-time Ann Arbor Spark FastTrack award, Crain’s Detroit Business Cool Places to Work in Michigan, Detroit Free Press’ Top Workplace, Gold Best in Biz Award for most Innovative Company of the year, CEO World Award, Golden Bridge Award, Stevie Awards and Edison Awards.

Larry has a B.S. in computer science from the University of Michigan and a M.B.A. from Wayne State University.

John Williams, General Partner

As SVP of Product and Services at ForeSee, John drove the development and commercial introduction of ForeSee’s next-generation product platform, providing greater strategic decision-making support and organizational leverage to ForeSee clients. He headed up the research team which drove ForeSee’s ongoing innovation in predictive analytics as well as overseeing ForeSee’s large client services organization, which helps clients efficiently deliver excellent customer experiences and thereby achieve superior business outcomes.

John’s extensive executive experience includes his tenure as Vice President of Research and Development at Micro Focus, where John headed a global software development organization that rationalized multiple acquired products and technologies into a coherent, high growth platform. As Senior Vice President of Product & Commercial Enablement at Compuware Corporation, John drove product and M&A initiatives that turned a floundering Application Performance Management business into a 30%+ growth engine that became Compuware’s flagship offering. He went on to restructure the company into focused business units – making it possible to optimize each business and divest underperforming product lines. At AT&T Bell Laboratories, John developed a roadmap for delivery of a next-generation solution combining processor and peripherals on a single silicon chip.

Over the course of his career, John has identified, evaluated, negotiated, performed due diligence, defined and executed commercial and product integration strategies for dozens of tactical and strategic acquisitions - as well as divesting and restructuring underperforming business units. He has aligned go-to-market and product initiatives to target focused market opportunities. He has redefined product positioning and messaging to resonate with the business priorities of senior decision makers - resulting in greater sales productivity and scalability.

Aaron Watson, General Partner

Most recently, Aaron served as the Vice President of Business Development at ForeSee, where he led the strategic relationship strategy, including serving on the team that managed the successful acquisition of ForeSee by Answers.com. Additionally, he led efforts to build key alliances and partnerships that enhanced value delivery to customers, expand reach within relevant markets, and reaffirmed intellectual leadership within ForeSee's broader eco-system. He also oversaw the management of ForeSee’s rapidly growing partner community in order to expand the impact of customer experience analytics via the collaboration and integration of complementary products and services.

Prior to joining ForeSee, Aaron served as Vice President of Sales at DigiCert, one of the world’s largest online authentication and certificate authorities, which doubled revenue during his tenure, ultimately culminating in the successful acquisition of DigiCert by TA Associates. Aaron also served as Sr. Director of Strategic Alliances for Omniture, where he lead an alliance strategy resulting in a community of over 250 partners and 10,000 developers, which in turn enabled multiple acquisitions and led to Omniture’s recognition as the fastest growing SaaS company in the world. Aaron worked on the leadership team for successful corporate development activities such as an IPO as well as the acquisition of Omniture by Adobe, where he also served as Sr. Director of Strategic Alliances for a time.

Aaron has also held operations management positions at ADP Benefit Services and GE Capital and has had the opportunity to do business in many countries and with many cultures all over the world.

Mr. Watson, a dynamic speaker, earned his MBA from the David Eccles School of Business at the University of Utah where he also completed his undergraduate work in Mass Communication and Public Relations. He lives in Utah.

Cia McCaffrey, General Partner

As a founding member of the ForeSee executive team in 2001, Cia grew the company to over 300 employees and developed hiring and training practices that created a cohesive, passionate team of the most talented professionals in the world, which was instrumental to the company’s financial success and lucrative exit. More specifically, Cia built a determined and scalable sales force, implementing structural organization models, motivational compensation plans, and a rigorous training program, all resulting in a high powered, motivated, and successful sales team. Across other departments at ForeSee, Cia partnered with each division head to create scalable organization structures with the greatest opportunity for investment and growth. By creating positive communication methods she was able to create a collaborative team environment where employees feel comfortable contributing at their highest potential, because her philosophy is that employees are a company’s strongest asset.

Previous to joining ForeSee, Cia was with Compuware where she was Director of Human Resources and Recruiting. She was able to lead the specialty practice by building a team of talented individuals as well as integrating acquired resources for mutual maximum benefit. The practice grew to 400 employees within two years.

Her other experience includes playing critical roles in other organizations such as American SCI (Ajilon) and CBSI (Covansys) where she led the recruiting practice and was able to use creative resourcing to hire the best and the brightest in a rapid business growth model. Her efforts impacted company value and contributed to successful acquisitions.
